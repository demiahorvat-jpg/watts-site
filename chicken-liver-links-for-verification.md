## Key Nutritional Comparisons (USDA Data)

### Chicken Liver vs Beef Liver per 100g:

| Nutrient | Chicken Liver | Beef Liver | Winner |
|----------|--------------|------------|---------|
| **Iron** | 10.12 mg | 6.12 mg | Chicken (65% more) |
| **Vitamin A** | 2,808 µg RAE | 7,683 µg RAE | Beef (2.7x more) |
| **Vitamin B12** | 15.07 µg | 82.47 µg | Beef (5.5x more) |
| **Folate** | 575 µg | 258 µg | Chicken (2.2x more) |
| **Copper** | 0.57 mg | 14.472 mg | Chicken (25x less - safer) |
| **Selenium** | 63.2 µg | 32.5 µg | Chicken (95% more) |
| **Fat** | 8.69g | 4.64g | Beef (lower fat) |
| **Protein** | 20.02g | 26.31g | Beef (higher protein) |

### Key Takeaways:
- **Chicken liver** = Better for iron, folate, selenium; MUCH safer for copper-sensitive breeds
- **Beef liver** = Better for vitamin A, B12, protein; lower fat
- Both excellent, complementary nutrient profiles

## Content Highlights for Chicken Liver

**Unique selling points:**
1. **Highest iron content** - 10.12mg per 100g (vs 6.12mg in beef liver)
2. **Lowest copper** - 0.57mg per 100g (vs 14.472mg in beef liver) - CRITICAL for breeds with copper storage disease
3. **Exceptional folate** - 575µg per 100g (important for growth, pregnancy)
4. **Lower vitamin A** - Reduces toxicity risk compared to beef liver
5. **More affordable** - Practical for regular supplementation
6. **Must be cooked** - Unlike some beef liver preparations, chicken should be cooked

**Target audience:**
- Owners of Bedlington Terriers, Dobermans, Labs (copper-sensitive breeds)
- Dogs with anemia or iron deficiency
- Pregnant/nursing dogs (high folate needs)
- Budget-conscious owners seeking nutrient-dense options
- Anyone wanting to rotate between liver types
