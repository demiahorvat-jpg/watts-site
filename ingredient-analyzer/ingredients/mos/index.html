What It Is

Prebiotic fiber derived from yeast cell walls that supports immune function and blocks pathogen binding in the gut.

Compare to Similar Ingredients

  • vs. fructooligosaccharides: MOS (mannan-oligosaccharides) blocks pathogen binding AND feeds good bacteria, while FOS (fructooligosaccharides) primarily feeds beneficial bacteria. MOS has dual immune and prebiotic benefits; FOS is prebiotic-focused.
  • vs. inulin: MOS blocks pathogen adhesion to gut walls, while inulin is fermented by beneficial bacteria. MOS has unique immune benefits plus prebiotic effects; inulin is purely prebiotic fiber.
  • vs. dried chicory root: Chicory root is a whole food providing inulin (prebiotic fiber), while MOS is extracted from yeast cell walls with pathogen-blocking properties. MOS is more targeted functional ingredient; chicory is whole food.
  • vs. dried yeast: MOS is extracted from yeast cell walls (purified mannan compounds), while dried yeast provides broader nutritional benefits including B vitamins and protein. MOS is targeted prebiotic; yeast is multi-functional.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Manufacturers include mos in dog food, treats, and supplements for several reasons:

  • Prebiotic for beneficial bacteria
  • Blocks pathogen binding to gut wall
  • Immune system support
  • Gut health

Quality Considerations

When evaluating mos in dog products, it's important to understand soluble versus insoluble fiber, digestive health benefits, and stool quality. This ingredient's quality and appropriateness can vary significantly based on sourcing, processing, and the specific formula it's used in.

Quality Note

Well-researched prebiotic with immune benefits. Binds pathogens like E. coli and Salmonella, preventing colonization. Derived from yeast (Saccharomyces cerevisiae) cell walls. Quality depends on extraction and concentration.

Potential Concerns

While mos can be appropriate in dog nutrition, pet owners should be aware of excessive fiber reducing nutrient absorption and causing digestive upset. Individual dogs may respond differently to the same ingredient based on their health status, age, and sensitivities.

Watts' Take

Excellent functional prebiotic with dual benefits - feeds good bacteria AND blocks bad bacteria. MOS has strong research for gut and immune health. Premium ingredient indicating attention to digestive wellness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is mos safe for dogs?

Yes, mos is safe for dogs when used appropriately in properly formulated dog food. As with any ingredient, individual dogs may have sensitivities, so monitor for any adverse reactions when first introducing products containing this ingredient.

What does mos do in dog products?

Prebiotic fiber derived from yeast cell walls that supports immune function and blocks pathogen binding in the gut. Dog food manufacturers include this ingredient to provide prebiotic for beneficial bacteria and provide blocks pathogen binding to gut wall.
