What It Is

Egg product is dried whole eggs providing complete protein in dog food. Whole eggs (before drying) contain approximately 75% moisture, 13g protein, 11g fat, minimal carbohydrates per 100g. Once dried during processing, egg product becomes concentrated protein source with approximately 45-50% protein, 40-45% fat. Eggs provide the most complete and bioavailable protein available—containing all essential amino acids in optimal ratios (biological value of 100, the gold standard). Eggs are rich in vitamins A, D, E, B-complex (especially B12, riboflavin, folate), minerals (iron, selenium, zinc), choline (brain health), and lutein/zeaxanthin (eye health). Highly digestible (95%+) and hypoallergenic (egg allergies rare). Egg product is premium ingredient signaling quality formulation.

Compare to Similar Ingredients

  • vs. chicken meal: Egg product and chicken meal are both complete animal proteins. Eggs have higher biological value (100 vs 85-90) and more balanced amino acids. Chicken meal provides more protein per weight (60-65% vs 45-50%). Eggs are more expensive and provide more fat. Both are excellent—eggs are gold standard protein; chicken meal is economical complete protein.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Egg product appears in dog food as premium complete protein with highest biological value. It provides all essential amino acids in optimal ratios. Eggs are highly digestible (95%+), hypoallergenic, and nutrient-dense with vitamins, minerals, and choline. Premium positioning ingredient—signals quality and completeness. Eggs support muscle maintenance, cognitive function, eye health, and overall nutrition. More expensive than most proteins, limiting use to premium formulas. Often combined with meat proteins for variety.

How to Spot on Labels

Egg product appears as "egg product," "liquid egg product," or "whole egg product" (fresh, not dried). Distinct from "dried egg product" (concentrated) and "egg whites" (albumen only).

Alternative Names

  • Whole egg product
  • Liquid eggs
  • Fresh eggs
  • Liquid egg product

Positioning on Labels

Fresh egg product appears higher on labels than dried egg due to water weight. Common in wet food and fresh/frozen formulas. In dry kibble, dried egg product is more common than fresh egg product (moisture removed during processing).

Red Flags

  • Egg product high on label but surrounded by low-quality proteins (meat meal, meat by-products) = eggs used for marketing

Green Flags

  • Egg product paired with named proteins (chicken, beef, fish)
  • Egg product in wet food or fresh formulas where moisture is appropriate
  • Organic or free-range eggs specified

Quality Indicators

Egg product signals quality and digestibility focus. Premium wet foods and fresh diets use eggs as supplemental protein. For allergy-prone dogs, eggs are generally well-tolerated and provide excellent amino acid profile. Watch for formulas pairing eggs with limited ingredients for hypoallergenic diets or easy digestion for puppies and seniors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between 'egg product' and 'dried egg'?

"Egg product" typically refers to whole eggs that may be fresh, liquid, or lightly processed, while "dried egg" or "dried egg product" is dehydrated whole eggs in powder form. Both contain the complete egg (whites and yolks). Dried egg is more concentrated—about 45-50% protein versus 13% in fresh eggs due to water removal. Fresh eggs appear higher on ingredient lists due to water weight but contribute less actual protein than dried egg listed in the same position.

Why do eggs have the highest biological value of any protein?

Eggs score a perfect 100 on the biological value scale—the gold standard by which all other proteins are measured. This is because eggs contain all essential amino acids in the exact ratios dogs need, with 95%+ digestibility. For comparison, chicken has a biological value of 79, beef is 80, and most plant proteins are 50-70. The amino acid profile in eggs is so complete that nutritionists use it as the reference standard for evaluating other protein sources.

Are eggs a common allergen for dogs?

No, eggs are one of the least allergenic protein sources for dogs. True egg allergies are rare—much less common than chicken, beef, or dairy allergies. This makes egg product useful in limited ingredient diets and for dogs with multiple food sensitivities. The protein structure of eggs is well-tolerated by most dogs. If your dog has been struggling with other proteins, egg-based foods are often recommended as an alternative.
