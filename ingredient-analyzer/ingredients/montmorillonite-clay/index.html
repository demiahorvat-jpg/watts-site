Scientific Evidence

Function and Purpose

Primary Function: Natural clay for digestive support and toxin binding

Nutritional Profile and Composition

Montmorillonite is a type of smectite clay with a layered structure that expands when hydrated. This expansion creates high surface area for adsorption—the clay can bind toxins, bacteria, and other substances in the digestive tract. Montmorillonite also contains trace minerals including calcium, magnesium, and silica.

The clay's structure allows it to bind positively charged molecules (cations) including bacterial toxins, mycotoxins, and some heavy metals. When passing through the digestive tract, montmorillonite can help reduce the impact of certain toxins by preventing their absorption.

Efficacy and Research

Research demonstrates that montmorillonite clay can reduce diarrhea duration and severity by binding bacterial toxins and supporting intestinal barrier function. Studies in dogs show benefits for acute diarrhea and as supportive therapy for digestive upset. The clay creates a protective coating on the intestinal lining.

Appropriate dosages range from 0.5-2g per 10kg body weight daily for acute digestive issues. Long-term use is less established—the clay can potentially bind nutrients and medications, reducing their absorption. Montmorillonite should be given separately from medications (2+ hours apart) and isn't appropriate for chronic daily use without veterinary guidance.

Evidence Rating

Moderate-to-Strong - Evidence supports short-term use for diarrhea; potential nutrient binding limits long-term application

Watts' Take

Beneficial detox ingredient in appropriate amounts. Montmorillonite clay binds toxins but can also bind nutrients if overused. Good in small amounts for digestive support. Quality and sourcing matter for purity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is montmorillonite clay?

Montmorillonite is a natural clay mineral (a type of bentonite) with unique properties that allow it to bind toxins, bacteria, and heavy metals in the digestive tract. It's been used therapeutically for centuries and is included in some pet foods for digestive support and natural mineral content.

Does montmorillonite clay help with diarrhea in dogs?

Montmorillonite clay can help firm up loose stools by absorbing excess water and binding bacterial toxins in the gut. It's sometimes used therapeutically during digestive upset. However, the amounts in regular dog food are typically for mineral content rather than therapeutic effect.

Is montmorillonite clay safe for dogs?

Yes, food-grade montmorillonite clay is safe for dogs. It provides trace minerals and has been used safely in animal feed for decades. Quality matters—reputable brands use purified, tested clay free from contaminants. It passes through the digestive system without being absorbed into the body.
