What It Is

Crab meal is rendered, dried crab (including shell and meat) ground into a protein-rich meal used as a novel protein source in pet foods. Contains natural glucosamine for joint support.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Manufacturers include crab meal in dog food, treats, and supplements for several reasons:

  • Novel protein source for elimination diets
  • Concentrated protein content
  • Contains chitin from shells (fiber and glucosamine)
  • Rich in minerals like calcium, selenium, and zinc
  • Provides omega-3 fatty acids

Quality Considerations

When evaluating crab meal in dog products, it's important to understand protein density, amino acid profile, digestibility, and sourcing quality. This ingredient's quality and appropriateness can vary significantly based on sourcing, processing, and the specific formula it's used in.

Quality Note

Crab meal is a concentrated protein source that includes both meat and shell material. The shell contributes chitin (a type of fiber) and glucosamine, while the meat provides protein and minerals. The inclusion of shells means lower meat protein compared to whole meat, but adds beneficial compounds. Quality varies depending on the ratio of meat to shell. It's a novel protein useful for dogs with common protein allergies. The chitin is indigestible but may offer prebiotic benefits.

Potential Concerns

While crab meal can be appropriate in dog nutrition, pet owners should be aware of allergies, protein digestibility, and quality variations between named and generic sources. Individual dogs may respond differently to the same ingredient based on their health status, age, and sensitivities.

Watts' Take

Crab meal is a decent novel protein option with some unique benefits from the shell material (glucosamine, chitin). However, because it includes shells, you're getting less actual meat protein and more indigestible material compared to shrimp meal or fish meal. It's acceptable as a secondary protein or novel protein source, but we'd prefer named fish meals or whole proteins as primary sources. The glucosamine content is a bonus. Overall, it's fine but not exceptional.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is crab meal safe for dogs?

Yes, crab meal is safe for dogs when used appropriately in properly formulated dog food. As with any ingredient, individual dogs may have sensitivities, so monitor for any adverse reactions when first introducing products containing this ingredient.

What does crab meal do in dog food?

Crab meal is rendered, dried crab (including shell and meat) ground into a protein-rich meal used as a novel protein source in pet foods. Dog food manufacturers include this ingredient to provide novel protein source for elimination diets and provide concentrated protein content.

Can crab meal cause allergies in dogs?

Any protein source can potentially trigger allergies in sensitive dogs. Signs of food allergies include itchy skin, ear infections, digestive issues, and excessive paw licking. If your dog shows these symptoms, consult your veterinarian about an elimination diet to identify the trigger ingredient.

How much crab meal should be in dog food?

Quality dog foods typically list primary protein sources like crab meal within the first 3-5 ingredients. The exact percentage varies by formula, but protein-focused foods generally contain 25-35% total protein for adults, and 28-40% for puppies or performance dogs. Check the guaranteed analysis rather than relying solely on ingredient order.
