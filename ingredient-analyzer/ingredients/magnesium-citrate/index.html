What It Is

Magnesium citrate is a highly bioavailable form of magnesium used to supplement pet foods for muscle, nerve, and enzyme function.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Manufacturers include magnesium citrate in dog food, treats, and supplements for several reasons:

  • Highly absorbable magnesium source
  • Essential for muscle and nerve function
  • Required for energy metabolism
  • Supports bone health and enzyme activity
  • Gentler on digestive system than oxide forms

Scientific Evidence

Function and Purpose

Primary Function: Highly bioavailable magnesium source for supplementation

Nutritional Profile and Composition

Magnesium citrate is magnesium bound to citric acid, creating a chelated mineral supplement. Magnesium is an essential mineral cofactor for over 300 enzymatic reactions, including energy metabolism, protein synthesis, muscle and nerve function, and bone health. The citrate form offers superior bioavailability compared to magnesium oxide or sulfate.

Magnesium plays critical roles in ATP production, DNA and RNA synthesis, muscle contraction and relaxation, nerve transmission, and regulation of calcium and potassium channels. Deficiency can impair numerous physiological processes, though it's rare in dogs fed complete commercial diets.

Efficacy and Research

Research demonstrates that magnesium citrate has higher absorption rates (30-40%) compared to magnesium oxide (4-15%), making it a preferred supplemental form. Most dogs receive adequate magnesium from standard diets, but supplementation may benefit dogs with increased needs (athletic dogs, certain medical conditions) or those on therapeutic diets.

At higher doses, magnesium citrate has mild laxative effects due to osmotic activity in the intestines, which can be therapeutic for constipation but problematic if excessive. Typical supplementation ranges from 15-30mg per kg body weight daily. Magnesium works synergistically with calcium, vitamin D, and B vitamins.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why use magnesium citrate instead of other magnesium forms?

Magnesium citrate is highly bioavailable—the citrate bond makes it easier to absorb than magnesium oxide or other forms. This means dogs can use more of the magnesium provided, requiring smaller amounts to meet nutritional needs. It's generally gentler on the digestive system than some other forms.

What does magnesium do for dogs?

Magnesium is essential for over 300 enzymatic reactions including muscle and nerve function, energy production, bone development, and heart health. Deficiency can cause muscle weakness, tremors, and abnormal heart rhythms. Most dogs get adequate magnesium from balanced commercial food.

Can dogs get too much magnesium?

Healthy dogs with normal kidney function can excrete excess magnesium, making toxicity rare from food sources. However, dogs with kidney disease may accumulate magnesium and need controlled intake. High supplemental magnesium can cause diarrhea. Commercial dog foods are formulated within safe ranges.
