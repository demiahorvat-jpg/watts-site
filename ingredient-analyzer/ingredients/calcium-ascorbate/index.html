What It Is

Buffered, non-acidic form of vitamin C (ascorbic acid) combined with calcium, used as both an antioxidant and vitamin supplement.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Manufacturers include calcium ascorbate in dog food, treats, and supplements for several reasons:

  • Natural antioxidant and preservative
  • Provides vitamin C for immune support
  • Less acidic than ascorbic acid, gentler on stomach
  • Dual benefit: vitamin C plus calcium
  • Supports collagen production and joint health

Quality Considerations

When evaluating calcium ascorbate in dog products, it's important to understand bioavailability, synthetic versus natural forms, and deficiency prevention. This ingredient's quality and appropriateness can vary significantly based on sourcing, processing, and the specific formula it's used in.

Quality Note

Dogs synthesize their own vitamin C, so supplementation is not essential like in humans. However, additional vitamin C may benefit senior dogs, those with joint issues, or during stress/illness. Calcium ascorbate is gentler than ascorbic acid and provides calcium bonus. Primarily used as preservative in pet food.

Potential Concerns

While calcium ascorbate can be appropriate in dog nutrition, pet owners should be aware of proper dosing, interactions with other nutrients, and toxicity from over-supplementation. Individual dogs may respond differently to the same ingredient based on their health status, age, and sensitivities.

Watts' Take
