Quality Considerations

When evaluating paractin in dog products, it's important to understand clinical evidence, appropriate dosing, and targeted health benefits. This ingredient's quality and appropriateness can vary significantly based on sourcing, processing, and the specific formula it's used in.

Quality Note

Branded, researched ingredient with clinical studies in both humans and animals. Standardized for andrographolide content (typically 30%). More reliable than generic andrographis due to standardization and quality control. Used in premium joint and immune supplements.

Scientific Evidence

Paractin is a branded, standardized extract from the plant Andrographis paniculata, traditionally used in Ayurvedic and Chinese medicine. It's primarily marketed for joint health and immune support, with research focusing on its anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties.

Key Research Findings

  • Paractin is standardized to contain specific levels of andrographolides, the primary bioactive compounds responsible for its anti-inflammatory effects
  • Research in humans and laboratory studies suggests andrographolides may inhibit pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1β) and modulate immune cell activity
  • Studies have shown potential benefits for joint discomfort and mobility in human trials, though canine-specific research is limited
  • May support immune function through modulation of T-cell and macrophage activity
  • Bioavailability can be variable; some formulations include absorption enhancers
  • Safety data in dogs is limited; most evidence comes from human use and general toxicology studies
  • Typical supplemental dosing in pet products ranges from 100-300mg per day based on body weight, though optimal canine dosing is not well-established

Evidence Level: Moderate evidence from human and in vitro studies for anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating effects. Limited direct canine research. Safety profile appears favorable but not extensively studied in dogs.

How to Spot on Labels

What to Look For

Paractin is a branded ingredient found exclusively in joint health supplements and immune support products. Since it's a proprietary extract with standardized potency, its presence indicates a formulator has chosen a research-backed botanical ingredient. Look for dosage information to assess meaningful inclusion.

Alternative Names

  • Paractin — Branded/trademarked name (standard listing)
  • Andrographis paniculata extract — May appear as generic listing
  • Andrographis extract — Shortened form

Green Flags

  • Listed with standardization (e.g., "Paractin [standardized to 30% andrographolides]") — Confirms quality-controlled potency
  • Specified dosage per serving on label — Indicates therapeutic intent with measurable inclusion
  • In joint support or immune health supplements — Appropriate use case based on mechanism of action
  • Combined with other joint ingredients — Glucosamine, chondroitin, MSM, turmeric create comprehensive support

What's Normal

Paractin is a premium ingredient typically found in higher-end joint supplements. Since it's a branded extract, its inclusion suggests formulator attention to evidence-based ingredients, though canine-specific research remains limited. Dosage matters significantly for effectiveness.

Typical Position: In joint supplements, Paractin typically appears in positions 5-12, reflecting meaningful therapeutic inclusion.

Watts' Take

Premium branded ingredient with solid research backing for inflammation and immune support. Standardization ensures consistent potency. More expensive than generic herbs but quality and efficacy are verified. Good choice for joint supplements and immune formulas.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Paractin in dog food?

Paractin is a patented, standardized extract from the Andrographis paniculata plant. It contains compounds called andrographolides that have been studied for their anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating properties. It's primarily found in joint supplements and senior dog formulas.

What are the benefits of Paractin for dogs?

Research suggests Paractin may help reduce inflammation and support joint comfort, making it popular in formulas for aging or active dogs. Some studies indicate it may also support immune function. Unlike NSAIDs, it works through natural anti-inflammatory pathways.

Is Paractin safe for dogs?

Paractin has been studied for safety in dogs and is generally well-tolerated at recommended doses. Because it's a standardized extract, dosing is consistent between products that use it. As with any supplement, it's best used under veterinary guidance, especially for dogs on medications or with health conditions.
