Quick Summary

What It Is

Natural acid used as preservative and pH regulator. Produced by fermentation.

Compare to Similar Ingredients

  • vs. citric acid: Both are acidulants and preservatives. Lactic acid is from bacterial fermentation with milder flavor, while citric acid is from mold/citrus with sharper taste.

Why It's Used in Dog Products

Manufacturers include lactic acid in dog food, treats, and supplements for several reasons:

  • Natural preservative
  • pH regulation
  • Antimicrobial properties

Function and Purpose

Organic acid used as preservative, pH buffer, and digestive support agent. Functions through antimicrobial activity and pH reduction.

Bioavailability and Absorption

Rapidly metabolized via Cori cycle and pyruvate pathway. Well-tolerated at food preservation levels.

Efficacy and Benefits

Effective antimicrobial at food preservation concentrations. May support digestive health through lactate metabolism and prebiotic effects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is lactic acid considered a good ingredient?

Lactic acid is rated Good because it's a natural preservative produced by bacterial fermentation. It has antimicrobial properties, helps regulate pH, and is safer than synthetic preservatives. Dogs metabolize lactic acid through normal pathways (the Cori cycle), making it well-tolerated. It's the same acid found naturally in fermented foods like yogurt and kefir.

Where should lactic acid appear on the ingredient list?

Lactic acid typically appears very late in ingredient lists (positions 25-40), within the preservative section. Only small amounts are needed for pH regulation and antimicrobial preservation. If lactic acid appeared high on an ingredient list, it would indicate unusual amounts—this would be unexpected since preservatives are used in small quantities.

Is lactic acid necessary in dog food?

No, lactic acid is a preservative and pH regulator, not a nutrient. Dogs don't need it nutritionally. It serves functional purposes: lowering pH to inhibit bacterial growth, extending shelf life, and potentially supporting digestive health through its mild prebiotic effects. It's a safe, effective preservation tool that's preferable to synthetic alternatives.
