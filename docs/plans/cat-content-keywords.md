## Hub Page: Cat Health

**Target keyword:** "cat health" (broad — hub serves as navigation/positioning piece)

**Supporting keywords:**
- feline health
- cat wellness
- keeping cats healthy

**Intent:** Informational — cat owners seeking general health guidance

**Strategy:** Position as authoritative overview linking to specific pillar guides. Don't try to rank for ultra-competitive head terms; instead, capture long-tail through pillar content.

## Pillar 1: Feline Immune Health

### Guide Keywords

**Primary:** "cat immune system"
**Secondary:**
- how the cat immune system works
- feline immune system
- cat immunity

**Title angle:** "Complete Guide to the Feline Immune System: How It Works & What Supports It"

### Blog Post Cluster

| Post Topic | Primary Keyword | Search Intent | Notes |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|-------|
| Immune support supplements | "immune support for cats" | Commercial/Info | High competition from brands (VetriScience, Hill's, etc.) — differentiate with biology-first approach |
| How to boost immunity | "how to boost cat immune system" | Informational | Top ranking: Catster, RAWZ, PetHonesty — can beat with more comprehensive, research-backed content |
| Vitamins for immunity | "vitamins for cat immune system" | Commercial/Info | Good opportunity — current content is thin |
| Beta glucans | "beta glucans for cats" | Informational | Lower competition, directly relevant to supplement positioning |

### Competitor Analysis

**Top ranking content for "cat immune system":**
- Hill's Pet: Educational but brand-focused
- Catster: List-format "8 tips" style
- VCA Hospitals: Clinical/veterinary tone

**Opportunity:** No comprehensive, biology-first guide that explains HOW the immune system works before jumping to tips. Watts can own this angle.

## SEO Notes

### Title Tag Formulas
- Guides: "Complete Guide to [Topic]: [Benefit/Angle] | Watts"
- Blog posts: "[Topic]: [Specific angle or question answered] | Watts"

### Meta Description Formula
- Lead with value/answer, include primary keyword, end with implicit CTA
- Example: "Learn how your cat's immune system works and what actually supports immune function. Research-backed guidance for cat owners."

### Schema Markup (all content)
- Article schema (BlogPosting for posts, Article for guides)
- FAQ schema where applicable
- BreadcrumbList schema

### Internal Linking Strategy
- Every blog post links to its parent pillar guide
- Pillar guides link to each other where relevant
- Hub page links to all pillar guides
- Existing cat content updated to link to new guides

## Competitive Gaps to Exploit

1. **Biology-first content** — Most competitors jump straight to tips/products. Watts explains how things work first.

2. **No dog comparisons** — Competitors often frame cat content relative to dogs. Watts treats cats as the main character.

3. **Health supplement angle** — Most content is from pet food brands (nutrition focus) or supplement brands (product focus). Watts bridges biology + practical support.

4. **Research-backed but accessible** — Vet sites are too clinical; pet blogs are too shallow. Watts hits the middle.
