## Key Findings

### Successfully Linked Ingredients
The following ingredients had natural mentions in blog content and received links:
- **Anchovies** (17 links) - Frequently mentioned in omega-3 and fish oil discussions
- **Yeast fermentate** (37 links) - Extensively discussed in dedicated blog post
- **Yeast beta-glucans** (21 links) - Well-covered in immune support and yeast-specific posts
- **Whitefish** (2 links) - Mentioned in digestive health contexts
- **Duck liver** (2 links) - Mentioned as vitamin A source
- **Pectin** (1 link) - Mentioned in gut health discussion
- **Ginger** (1 link) - Mentioned in supplement label reading context

### Ingredients Not Found in Current Content
Many specialized ingredients were not naturally mentioned in the current blog content:
- Most herbs/botanicals (ashwagandha, slippery elm, quercetin, sea buckthorn)
- Most beans/legumes (navy beans, yellow peas, split peas, pinto beans)
- Most fats/oils (olive oil, lamb fat, duck fat, palm fruit oil, menhaden oil)
- Some organ meats (lung)
- Some yeasts (hydrolyzed yeast)
- Most fibers (sweet potato fiber, lignocellulose)
- Functional ingredients (paractin)

### Reasons for Low Link Counts
1. **Specialized ingredients** - Many of these ingredients are niche and only appear in specific product formulations
2. **Blog content focus** - Current blog content focuses on more common ingredients (salmon, sardines, beef liver, etc.)
3. **Ingredient list context** - Some ingredients primarily appear in product ingredient lists rather than blog discussions
4. **Alternative terminology** - Some ingredients might be discussed using different terms

## Link Quality Assessment

All links added were:
- ✅ Contextually relevant
- ✅ In meaningful content (paragraphs, lists, tables)
- ✅ Not in navigation, meta tags, or schema markup
- ✅ First occurrences in each content section to avoid overlinking
- ✅ Natural mentions, not forced
